Arepas

Description

Arepas are a South American dish, especially popular in Colombia and Venezuela, made from cornmeal and often stuffed with various fillings. They are essencially round, unleavened corn cakes that can be grilled, baked, or fried.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups pre-cooked cornmeal (such as P.A.N)
  • vegetable oil as needed

Steps

  1. Mix

    2. Stir water and salt together ina medium bowl; gradually stir in cornmeal with your fingers until the mixture forms a soft, moist, malleable dough.

  2. Shape

    3. Form dough into eight 2-inch diameter balls; pat each ball to flatten into a 3/8-inch-thick arepa patty.

  3. Cook

    4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat; fry arepas in batches until golden brown, about 4 to 5 minutes per side. Transfer cooked arepas to a paper towel-lined plate to drain until cool enough to handle.

  4. Prepare to Enjoy!

    5. Slice halfway horizontally with a thin knife to form a pita-like pocket to stuff with your favorite filling. In Venezuela people usually put butter as the base and work their way up! Anything goes, be creative!
